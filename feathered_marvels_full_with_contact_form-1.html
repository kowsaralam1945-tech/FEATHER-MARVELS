Did You Know?

Birds are some of the most fascinating creatures on Earth. With over 10,000 species, they have adapted to nearly every environment. Here are a few mind-blowing facts about our avian friends.

🦅
Speed Demon

The Peregrine Falcon

While the cheetah is the fastest animal on land, the Peregrine Falcon wins the race in the air. When diving for prey, it can reach speeds over 200 miles per hour (320 km/h)!

🦜
Brain Power

The Lyrebird

Found in Australia, the Superb Lyrebird is the world’s best mimic. It can imitate the sounds of over 20 other species, including chainsaws, car engines, and camera shutters perfectly.

🐧
Aquatic Skill

Penguins: Birds That Swim

You might think Penguins can't fly, but they are essentially flightless birds adapted for swimming. The Gentoo penguin is actually the fastest swimmer in the bird world, reaching speeds of 22 mph (36 km/h).

🐦
Tiny Titan

The Hummingbird

Hummingbirds are the smallest birds in the world, but they have the biggest hearts (proportionally). Their wings beat 80 times per second, and they are the only birds that can fly backwards!

🦉
Night Vision

Rotating Heads

Owls have extremely flexible necks. They can turn their heads up to 270 degrees (almost backwards!) without moving their shoulders, giving them incredible hunting vision.

🐤
Migration

The Arctic Tern

The Arctic Tern has the longest migration route of any animal in the world. It flies from its breeding grounds in the Arctic to winter in the Antarctic—roughly 25,000 miles roundtrip every year.

More Bird Facts

Birds have incredible adaptations and behaviors.

  • Many birds use Earth's magnetic field for navigation.
  • Parrots can solve puzzles and remember patterns.
  • Some birds sleep with one half of the brain awake.
  • Ostriches are the largest living birds.
